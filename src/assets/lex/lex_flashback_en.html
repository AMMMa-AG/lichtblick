flashback: A shot or a series of shots that break normal chronological order by shifting directly to an event representing the past. Flashbacks are jumps in diegetic time. They may be subjective (showing the thoughts and memory of a character, see mindscreen) or objective (returning to earlier events to show their relationship to the present). Flashbacks may often make up almost the entire film (e.g. a character tells his life story in a flashback). Usually, the beginning of a flashback is clearly marked with various stylistic devices such as, dissolves, fades and superimpositions. A larger pattern of cutting back and forth between the present time in film, flashbacks and flash-forwards may be used in parallel editing. The opposite of a flashback, the jump to future events, is called flash-forward.
