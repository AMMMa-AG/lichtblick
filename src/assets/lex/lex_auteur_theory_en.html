auteur theory: A theory that states that a director’s films reflect that particular director’s personal creative and artistic vision. In line with this theory, the director is regarded as the “auteur” (French for “author”) of a film because he/she has primary control and responsibility for the final product. A basic tenor of auteur theory is that a film should be considered in terms of the entire canon of films made by a director displaying a unique cinematic style, and for this reason, the auteur theory has had an important impact on film criticism. In particular, it is often associated with the French new wave and the film critics who wrote for the influential French film review periodical Cahiers du Cinéma.
