eye-level angle: A shot taken from the height of the subject’s eyes, in which the horizon line passes behind the subject’s eyes and directs the audience’s attention towards the main area of interest, namely the subject’s face and eyes. Shots filmed at eye-level are most commonly used to give the audience the impression of being on the same level as the subject because they are neither looking up nor down at it. In line with this, eye-level shots normally have a neutral connotation.
