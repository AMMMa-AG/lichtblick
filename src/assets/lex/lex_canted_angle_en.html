canted angle (oblique angle, Dutch angle): A shot with the camera tilted laterally (see also rotate) that generates a sloping horizon line and transforms formerly horizontal and vertical lines into diagonals. Put more simply, this means that a canted angle creates a view in which the frame is not level. First and foremost, canted angles distort the perspective and thereby often create an effect of confusion because the appearance of the mise en scène contradicts with the audience’s expectation. This, in turn, often gives the impression of a loss of control and a sense of imbalance. This unpleasant departure from the norm may be the reason that it is also usual to call the canted angle a Dutch angle (in line with other expressions in English of this type that are similarly negative, e.g. Dutch courage, to go Dutch, etc.).
