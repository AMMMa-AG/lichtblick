close-up: A part of the subject fills most of the frame, with little or no part of the surroundings. Typically, the image shows a face, sometimes also from the neck up or from the upper shoulder. The close-up emphasizes facial expressions, gestures, or the details of a significant object. The close-up can be used to evoke strong emotions in the viewers (and thus may lead to strong identification with characters). For related information, read the entry concerning camera distance.
