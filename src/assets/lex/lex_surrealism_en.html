surrealism (film): A European avant-garde film movement stretching from the 1920s to the 1930s. This movement was concerned with Freudian and Marxist ideas and is characterized by irrational settings and subject relations. A major film associated with the surrealist movement is Un chien andalou by Luis Buñuel and Salvador Dalí (1929). The surrealist movement, along with its ideas and techniques, is most commonly associated with the influence it had on many later experimental filmmakers. Nevertheless, even nowadays, many mainstream films and music videos also contain surrealistic elements.
