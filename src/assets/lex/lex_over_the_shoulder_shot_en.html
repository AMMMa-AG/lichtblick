over-the-shoulder shot (OTS): It is a kind of shot that is taken from over-the-shoulder of a character whose back can be partially seen (although not necessarily in sharp focus) at the side of the frame. Although over-the-shoulder shots could suggest a certain degree of subjectivity as the audience may identify with the person whose back is seen, they should not be confused with point-of-view shots. An over-the-shoulder shot helps to establish the relative positions of each character; it is thus helpful in creating spatial continuity. In addition, over-the-shoulder shots enhance the depth in a shot. A further important usage of over-the-shoulder shots is in shot/reverse shot patterns.
