Cinéma Vérité: Cinéma Vérité (French for “truthful cinema”) is a form of typically documentary-style filmmaking, combining naturalistic techniques with stylized cinematic devices of editing and cinematography which was developed in France in the 1960s. The Cinéma Vérité filmmakers made use of newly-developed, unobtrusive lightweight equipment for filming on location, and often tried to deliberately provoke the people they filmed by placing them in crisis situations to get at the truth that they felt was hidden under the surface. A similar movement arose in the United States at approximately the same time under the label of Direct Cinema. However, the direct cinema makers took more of a “fly on the wall”-observer approach to allow the truth to reveal itself. Aspects of Cinéma Vérité and direct cinema also later influenced the work of fictional filmmakers.
