realistic setting: The term “realism” is related to the representation of objects and conditions as they actually are in reality or how they were in the past. In keeping with this, a realistic setting involves the representation of living spaces or other locations in a form that is as close as possible to how they actually are or were. Often real locations are chosen as shooting locations for realistic settings, but it is also common to use especially-constructed sets. Whichever form is chosen, filmmakers always select settings and shooting locations in accordance with their special needs and requirements for their vision of how they want to realise the story. In this respect, even realistic settings show only a controlled selection of realistic conditions, and in addition, are often full of stereotypes that are being played with by the filmmaker or props and decoration that are designed to have specific effects on how the viewers see the scene. It is also possible for nonrealistic actions to take place in realistic settings. For related information, read the entry concerning the nonrealistic setting.
