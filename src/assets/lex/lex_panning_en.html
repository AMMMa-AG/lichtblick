panning: derived from panorama; a movement of the stationary camera, in which the camera moves along its horizontal axis, from right to left or vice versa. A shot in which substantial panning movement takes place is called a pan shot. See also tilting.

Pans have three main functions:
  1. to give an overview (panorama) of a scene,
  2. to direct attention from one subject or place to another,
  3. to follow movement or action.

A pan quick enough to blur the image is referred to as a swish pan. A pan in which the camera makes a single complete turn around itself is called 360-degree pan.
