body language: Body language includes facial expressions and other gestures that people use to instinctively communicate their emotions in a non-verbal way. While some of the body languages codes we use and understand are universal to all cultures, others, primarily those related to rules of behaviour, differ (sometimes quite radically) from culture to culture.

Examples:
Sweeping gestures which involve movement away from a person typically indicate an open attitude and sincerity, whereas gestures that are stilted and involve restricting movement closer to the person generally reveal uncertainty and inhibition. Raised shoulders and a lowering of the head can be a sign of anxiety or fear because the shoulders are there to protect the neck in the event of an attack. When totally relaxed, people hold their shoulders low so that the arms can move freely in a natural way without jerkiness.
