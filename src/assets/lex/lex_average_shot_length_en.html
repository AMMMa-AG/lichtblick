average shot length (ASL): The Average Shot Length (abbreviated ASL) of a film is a cinemetrical, statistical measurement which divides the total length of the film by the total number of shots in order to get an approximate idea of how long each shot lasts on average. The ASL in classical Hollywood films is typically about 9 seconds, whereas most modern action films have a significantly lower ASL, reflecting their fast-paced action (see the table for the ASL data of some selected films).
