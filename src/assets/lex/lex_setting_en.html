setting: Setting refers to the time and space where the action of a film takes place. The setting of a film can differ from the shooting location, i.e. the physical place where the filming of the action takes place. A distinction is made between shooting locations that are built specially for film shooting – this type is called a set – and real locations that are only changed slightly. Equipment and decoration of the shooting location can reveal a lot about the setting, or in other words, the time and space of the story. As a rule, both the setting and the appearance of the characters are designed to match each other. Further aspects may be discovered by looking for contrasts or exaggerated uniformity.
