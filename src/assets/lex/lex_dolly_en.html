dolly: A hydraulically-powered camera platform on wheels (sometimes also on rails) that allows for smooth and silent movements during filming. A dolly is often used to move toward (= “dolly in”, “move in”, “track in”) or away from (= “dolly out”, “dolly back”, “move out”, “track back”) the action. When the dolly-mounted camera moves alongside the subject, this is called a "tracking within" or "travelling shot". When the dolly moves away from the subject or object, the camera reveals further visual information that was previously out of the frame (e.g. other characters). It is important, however, to distinguish dolly in and dolly out shots from zoom shots.
