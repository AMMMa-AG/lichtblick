low-budget film: Films that are made independently of the mainstream film industry and that are only funded by a few independent producers or private persons, often have to deal with the restrictions of an especially low production budget. As a result, these films normally make do without costly directors, writers and actors and are usually shot at cheap locations or in independent studios. Further challenges that these productions have to deal include limited technical equipment, costumes and set decoration. Thus, filmmakers have to be especially inventive to realize their overall creative concept or idea. Additionally, a low-budget strongly influences the organization of the filming process, because every single day of shooting involves high costs for the shooting location or set, the technical equipment, as well as cast and crew. The first films of young directors and most independent films are typically low-budget productions.
