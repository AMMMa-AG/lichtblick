camera angle: The camera angle marks the perspective that the camera takes on the subject being shot. Optical distortions of the subject which arise from different camera angle perspectives often generate specific associations in terms of power or inferiority. Depending on the distance the camera has to the subject, the strength of such associations varies. For example, a close-up in combination with a low angle can often create a feeling of narrowness and claustrophobia; i.e. a loss of power or control. Nevertheless, such conventional associations can be adjusted, even to the point of acting as a parody or being contradictory if they are used in a particularly artful or unusual way.

Overhead angle, high angle, eye-level angle, low angle and below angle are the five basic angles; a special form of these is the canted angle, which also changes the angle of the framing and is often used in combination with other types of camera angles.
