high angle: A shot taken from above a characterâ€™s eye-level or more or less above the action. This perspective from above creates optical distortions of the subject or object that makes the top of it appear particularly large while the lower parts of it disappear under the enlarged top part and thus appear shorter. The audience, with its view from above, is in a privileged position, which is why a character, whose body is also compressed by this particular perspective, can seem helpless or vulnerable. In combination with close-ups, high angle shots can additionally create a sense of panic or claustrophobia.
