circle: The circle is a basic shape that can be used for compositional aspects within the frame. Circles can be implemented as a concrete shape formed by one or more objects of the mise en scène. In addition, circles can arise from cast shadows and lighting effects or even from non-physical connections between various faces, subjects or objects. They can also take the shape of a spiral which often creates a certain pull effect. Another circular shape is the ellipsis, which represents a flattened circle.
In contrast to the rectangle and due to its soft and smooth appearance, the circle is typically associated with natural and harmonic settings. A composition based on circles may suggest comforting security and harmonious closure, whereas the use of spirals often visually creates a whirling effect, which may hint at confusion or turmoil. A circle naturally focuses the viewer’s attention towards its centre, which is a point of special importance. Other basic shapes are lines, rectangles and triangles.
