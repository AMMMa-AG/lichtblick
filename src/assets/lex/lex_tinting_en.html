tinting: The process of adding colour to a black-and-white film. In the silent film era, the film was often tinted as a way of setting the mood. Scenes shot during the day were given a blue tint, which signified night. Red tints were used for scenes of fury, violence, heat or passion. Scenes tinted in green were generally mysterious or sea-faring scenes. A special form of selective tinting is hand colouring, which was often used in early fantasy films from Europe. In some black-and-white films, only a few frames were tinted for a surprising special effect.
