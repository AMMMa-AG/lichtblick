establishing shot: Usually the first shot of a scene. An establishing shot generally involves a distant framing that shows the overall spatial relations among the important figures and objects in a scene (most frequently, long shots and extreme long shots). The establishing shot, as the name indicates, establishes the setting of a scene, thereby introducing the viewers to a location and familiarizing them with the space relationships within it. If a later shot repeats the framing of the establishing shot, it is referred to as a re-establishing shot.
