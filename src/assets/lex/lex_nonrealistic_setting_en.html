nonrealistic setting: The term “nonrealism” is related to the representation of objects and conditions that have no reference to concrete objects or specific examples in reality. In line with this, a nonrealistic setting is the representation of living places or spaces that cannot be found in reality, whether present or past. Often such nonrealistic settings are shot in artificial shooting locations or sets. For example, a fairytale film including enchanted forests or caves and a witch’s cottage may be built specially for a film shooting. For related information, read the entry concerning realistic setting.
