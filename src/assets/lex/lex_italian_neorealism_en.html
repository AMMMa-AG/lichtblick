Italian neorealism: An Italian film movement of the 1940s and early 1950s, which was characterized by stories set amongst poor and working class people, generally filmed on location, and frequently made use of non-professional actors. The use of strongly realistic settings and techniques emphasized documentary aspects of film art. Italian neorealist films mostly dealt with the difficult economical and moral conditions of post-World War II Italy, critically reflecting the changes in the Italian society of the time and the hardships of everyday life: poverty, unemployment and desperation.
