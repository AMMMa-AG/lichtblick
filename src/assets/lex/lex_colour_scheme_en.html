colour scheme: This refers to a limited selection of colours. Normally, only a few, typically not more than three or four, and in most cases, more like two or three, basic hues are brought together to create harmonic images. From these basic colours, a variety of different tints and shades with varying degrees of saturation are then combined. In film, a frequently used colour scheme is that of monochromacy which uses only the nuances of a single colour. For related information, see the entries concerning analogue colours and triadic colours.
