computer-generated imagery (CGI): This is the application of computer-rendered graphics to special effects in films and television programs. Often, only a part of the frame is created via CGI (e.g. the background setting for astronauts floating in space); in other cases, the whole frame is computer generated. There are various reasons for using CGI visual effects. These include the fact that
  • computer-generated effects are easier to adjust and control than other more physically-based processes (e.g. crowd scenes) and real locations,
  • CGI allows the creation of images that would not be possible using analogue technology,
  • CGI allows a single graphic artist or small team to produce the desired content without the use of actors, expensive sets, or props.
