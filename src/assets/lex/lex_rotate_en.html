rotate (also called roll): A movement of a fixed camera around an imaginary axis that runs between the lens and the subject. The subject remains in place but the camera moves in a clockwise or counter-clockwise fashion. The rotation of the horizon line plays with the viewer’s everyday life experience of gravity (things look as if they might slide out of the frame). In this way, the rotation itself or the perspective it results in (see canted angle) helps to convey the topsy-turvy atmosphere of the setting. Nevertheless, it is worth noting that the rotation of a camera is less frequently used than other camera movements since it generally has a disorienting effect on the viewer.
