experimental film (avant-garde): A range of filmmaking styles that largely reject the conventions of mainstream films. Experimental films are often characterized by the absence of linear narrative and the presence of various abstracting techniques (e.g. filming out of focus, painting or scratching on film, fast-motion and slow-motion editing). The goal is often to put the viewer in a more active and reflective relationship to the film and the medium of film in general. Experimental filmmakers tend to favour style and form over subject matter as content.
