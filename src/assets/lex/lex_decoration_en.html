decoration: Refers to the visual design of the setting in which a story takes place. Furnishings, as well as wallpaper and many smaller objects, such as photos, pillows, bunches of flowers, blinds, lamps and bottles or cans placed within the mise en scène all belong to the decoration. The visual design of a setting may reveal a lot about the time and space of the story and a particular character’s personality. For example, large settings with a lot of decorative objects may reveal that the characters are wealthy, while dirty and narrow streets are typical of poorer districts.
